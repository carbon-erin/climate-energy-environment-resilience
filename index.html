Organizational Vision

Complex climate, energy, and environment challenges can rarely be solved by a single discipline, project, or organization.

Effective solutions often require reliable, technical expertise, stakeholder engagement, decision support, implementation, and evaluation.

We are exploring the formation of a nonprofit organization that brings a systems perspective to these challenges. We help connect information to understanding, understanding to action, and action to learning.

Our work may span an entire project lifecycle or focus on a specific need—from technical analysis and stakeholder engagement to facilitation, surveys, implementation support, and evaluation—while keeping broader goals and long-term outcomes in view.

Context

Why This Organization?

Challenges involving climate, energy, environmental resources, and community resilience are increasingly complex and interconnected.

Effective solutions often require reliable, technical expertise, stakeholder engagement, decision support, implementation, and evaluation.

Yet these capabilities are often fragmented across multiple organizations, making it difficult to move from understanding a challenge to acting on it.

We believe better decisions, and better outcomes, are possible when organizations understand the relationships among information, analysis, engagement, implementation, and learning.

This organization would bring a systems perspective to complex challenges. Our role may involve a full project lifecycle or a specific contribution, but we always seek to understand how each activity fits within a larger system and long-term objectives.

Purpose

Mission

To advance informed decisions about climate, energy, environmental resources, and community resilience through reliable data, technical analysis, stakeholder engagement, and practical decision support.

Approach

What We Do

We help decision-makers navigate complex climate, energy, environmental, and resilience challenges, from information and analysis through implementation, evaluation, and learning.

Open Access Data & Information

Develop and maintain datasets, maps, tools, and information resources that improve access to environmental and energy information.

Examples

  • Carbon management and CCS data
  • Regulatory information resources
  • Geospatial datasets
  • Environmental inventories
  • Resilience planning resources

Technical Analysis

Conduct and publish applied technical and economic analyses that support informed decision-making.

Examples

  • Carbon management
  • Environmental planning
  • Energy systems
  • Wildfire and resilience
  • Resource management
  • Geospatial analysis

Stakeholder Engagement

Design and facilitate processes that bring diverse perspectives together.

Examples

  • Public meetings
  • Community workshops
  • Surveys
  • Advisory processes
  • Collaborative planning

Decision Support

Create tools and resources that help decision-makers understand complex information and evaluate options.

Examples

  • Interactive maps
  • Dashboards
  • Scenario analysis
  • Visualization tools
  • Public-facing reports

Implementation & Evaluation

Support organizations as they move from planning to action, helping track progress, measure results, and learn from experience.

Examples

  • Implementation planning
  • Performance metrics
  • Monitoring programs
  • Program evaluation
  • Outcome tracking
  • Adaptive management

Framework

How We Work

We bring a systems perspective to complex climate, energy, environmental, and resilience challenges.

These challenges rarely fit neatly into a single discipline or activity. Effective solutions often require reliable, technical expertise, stakeholder engagement, decision support, implementation, and evaluation.

Some projects may require support throughout the entire process. Others may focus on a single activity, such as technical analysis, facilitation, surveys, decision support, or evaluation.

Regardless of our role, we seek to understand how each project fits within the broader system and contributes to long-term outcomes.

We help organizations move from information to understanding, understanding to action, and action to learning.

Focus

Potential Program Areas

Carbon & Energy Systems

Support informed decisions related to carbon management, energy systems, carbon capture and storage, emissions reduction, infrastructure planning, monitoring, reporting, and long-term stewardship.

We help stakeholders understand opportunities, risks, tradeoffs, and implementation pathways associated with evolving energy and carbon management systems.

Environment & Health

Support decisions that affect environmental quality, public health, natural resources, and community well-being through trusted information, technical analysis, and stakeholder engagement.

Topics may include air quality, water resources, environmental exposures, ecosystem impacts, environmental justice, and the relationships between environmental conditions and human health.

Resilience

Support resilience planning and implementation related to wildfire, climate adaptation, natural hazards, infrastructure, resource management, and long-term community resilience.

Our work focuses on helping organizations understand risks, evaluate options, prioritize actions, and measure progress over time.

Communities & Decision-Making

Support meaningful participation and informed decision-making through stakeholder engagement, facilitation, surveys, collaborative planning, and decision-support processes.

We help communities, agencies, researchers, and organizations work through complex challenges by building shared understanding and identifying actionable paths forward.
