Organizational Vision

A non-partisan, science-driven public-interest organization that develops information resources, technical analyses, geospatial tools, decision-support resources, and stakeholder-informed insights that help people navigate complex environmental and energy challenges.

We create useful resources—datasets, maps, analyses, and insights—so communities, agencies, researchers, and organizations can make more informed decisions.

Our mission is to advance informed decisions about climate, energy, environmental resources, and community resilience through reliable data, rigorous analysis, and practical resources.

Context

Why This Organization?

Communities, agencies, and organizations are increasingly asked to make complex decisions about energy systems, environmental resources, public health, and resilience. These decisions often involve technical, economic, regulatory, and social considerations, and they must frequently be made with limited time, resources, and certainty.

Expertise, data, and research are not in short supply. Universities, national laboratories, government agencies, nonprofits, and private organizations generate enormous amounts of information every year. Yet decision-makers often struggle to translate that knowledge into practical actions that reflect local priorities, constraints, and opportunities.

At the same time, many reports, models, dashboards, and planning efforts are developed without a clear path to implementation. Valuable lessons learned in one community may never reach another, and useful information can remain fragmented across organizations, disciplines, and sectors.

We believe there is a need for organizations that bridge the gap between expertise and action.

Our goal is to create useful information resources, technical analyses, geospatial tools, decision-support resources, and stakeholder-informed insights that help people navigate complex environmental and energy challenges. We are particularly interested in developing resources that are practical, accessible, and designed for the people who will ultimately use them.

Domains

Focus Areas

These are the application areas where our work focuses—challenges we address through research, resources, and tools. They are not rigid programs.

Carbon & Energy Systems

Applied research and resources related to carbon management, energy systems, and infrastructure.

Topics include

  • Carbon capture and storage
  • Emissions management
  • Energy infrastructure
  • Infrastructure planning
  • Monitoring
  • Reporting

Environment & Health

Applied research and resources examining relationships among environmental conditions, natural resources, public health, and community outcomes.

Topics include

  • Environmental quality
  • Natural resources
  • Public health
  • Environmental justice
  • Community well-being

Risk & Resilience

Applied research and resources that help people understand risks, evaluate options, and strengthen long-term resilience.

Topics include

  • Wildfire modeling
  • Hazard assessment
  • Climate adaptation
  • Infrastructure resilience
  • Long-term planning

Communities & Decision-Making

Applied research and resources that support collaborative understanding of issues, options, and pathways to action.

Topics include

  • Stakeholder engagement
  • Public participation
  • Surveys
  • Collaborative planning
  • Decision-support processes

Approach

How We Work

Environmental and energy challenges rarely fit within a single discipline or institution. Our work draws from experience across government, industry, academia, nonprofit organizations, and research institutions.

We bring together information, expertise, and stakeholder perspectives to create resources that are scientifically sound, practically useful, and grounded in real-world needs.
