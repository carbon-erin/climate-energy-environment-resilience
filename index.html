Formation Concept

Advancing informed decisions about climate, energy, environmental resources, and community resilience through trusted data, technical analysis, open information resources, and inclusive stakeholder engagement.

We are exploring the formation of a nonprofit organization focused on developing trusted information resources, conducting applied technical analysis, and supporting collaborative decision-making on complex environmental and energy challenges.

  • Founding board members
  • Advisors
  • Research collaborators
  • University and national laboratory partners

Context

Why This Organization?

Communities, agencies, researchers, and industry are increasingly asked to make decisions about complex and interconnected challenges involving climate, energy systems, natural resources, carbon management, and resilience.

These decisions often require:

  • Reliable and accessible data
  • Transparent technical analysis
  • Meaningful stakeholder participation
  • Collaboration across sectors
  • Trusted sources of information

At the same time, important information resources are frequently fragmented, difficult to access, or unavailable to the people who need them most.

We believe better decisions are possible when trusted information, rigorous analysis, and inclusive engagement are brought together.

Purpose

Mission

To advance informed decisions about climate, energy, environmental resources, and community resilience through trusted data, technical analysis, open information resources, and inclusive stakeholder engagement.

Approach

What We Do

Four complementary activities connect data, analysis, engagement, and decision support.

Open Information Resources

Develop and maintain datasets, maps, tools, and information resources that improve access to environmental and energy information.

Examples

  • Carbon management and CCS data
  • Regulatory information resources
  • Geospatial datasets
  • Environmental inventories
  • Resilience planning resources

Technical Analysis

Conduct and publish applied technical and economic analyses that support informed decision-making.

Examples

  • Carbon management
  • Environmental planning
  • Energy systems
  • Wildfire and resilience
  • Resource management
  • Geospatial analysis

Stakeholder Engagement

Design and facilitate processes that bring diverse perspectives together.

Examples

  • Public meetings
  • Community workshops
  • Surveys
  • Advisory processes
  • Collaborative planning

Decision Support

Create tools and resources that help decision-makers understand complex information and evaluate options.

Examples

  • Interactive maps
  • Dashboards
  • Scenario analysis
  • Visualization tools
  • Public-facing reports

Framework

How It Fits Together

Many organizations focus on only one part of the process.

Some produce data.
Some conduct research.
Some focus on outreach.

We believe the greatest value comes from connecting all three.

Focus

Potential Program Areas

Carbon Management

Carbon capture and storage, carbon accounting, monitoring, reporting, and environmental information resources.

Environmental Information

Geospatial datasets, mapping resources, environmental databases, and open information infrastructure.

Resilience

Wildfire resilience, climate adaptation, environmental risk, and long-term planning.

Stakeholder Processes

Public participation, surveys, facilitation, and collaborative decision-making.

Impact

Why This Matters

Many of society’s most complex challenges require both technical rigor and public trust.

We believe that trusted information, accessible data, transparent analysis, and inclusive engagement can help communities and decision-makers navigate these challenges more effectively.
