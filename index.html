Organizational Vision

Complex climate, energy, and environment challenges can rarely be solved by a single discipline, project, or organization.

Effective solutions often require reliable data, technical expertise, stakeholder engagement, decision support, implementation, and evaluation.

We are exploring the formation of a nonprofit organization that brings a systems perspective to these challenges. We believe better decisions and better outcomes are possible when information, expertise, stakeholder perspectives, implementation, and evaluation are considered together.

Our work may span an entire project lifecycle or focus on a specific need—from technical analysis and stakeholder engagement to facilitation, surveys, implementation support, and evaluation—while keeping broader goals and long-term outcomes in view.

Context

Why This Organization?

Challenges involving climate, energy, environmental resources, and community resilience are increasingly complex and interconnected.

Effective solutions often require reliable data, technical expertise, stakeholder engagement, decision support, implementation, and evaluation.

Yet these capabilities are often fragmented across multiple organizations, making it difficult to move from understanding a challenge to acting on it.

We believe better decisions and better outcomes are possible when information, analysis, engagement, implementation, and evaluation are considered together.

This organization would bring a systems perspective to complex challenges. Our role may involve a full project lifecycle or a specific contribution, but we always seek to understand how each activity fits within a larger system and long-term objectives.

Purpose

Mission

To advance informed decisions about climate, energy, environmental resources, and community resilience through reliable data, technical analysis, stakeholder engagement, and practical decision support.

Capabilities

How We Help

We help organizations navigate complex environmental and energy challenges by connecting information, expertise, and stakeholder perspectives.

Our capabilities can be applied across a wide range of projects, from focused technical studies and stakeholder engagement efforts to large, multi-disciplinary initiatives.

Open Access Information Resources

Develop and maintain datasets, maps, dashboards, information portals, tools, and other resources that improve access to environmental and energy information.

Our goal is not simply to provide information, but to make information useful and accessible to the people who need it.

Examples

  • Carbon management and CCS data
  • Geospatial datasets
  • Regulatory information resources
  • Interactive maps and dashboards
  • Public information portals

Technical Analysis

Conduct applied scientific, technical, economic, and geospatial analyses that support informed decision-making.

Examples

  • Carbon management
  • Energy systems
  • Environmental planning
  • Wildfire modeling
  • Risk assessment

Stakeholder Engagement

Design and facilitate processes that bring diverse perspectives together and support meaningful participation.

Examples

  • Public meetings
  • Community workshops
  • Surveys
  • Public participation GIS
  • Advisory processes
  • Collaborative planning
  • Facilitation

Decision Support

Help organizations understand complex issues, evaluate alternatives, identify tradeoffs, and make informed choices.

Examples

  • Scenario analysis
  • Interactive tools
  • Visualization products
  • Decision frameworks
  • Technical guidance
  • Public-facing reports

Implementation & Evaluation

Support organizations as they move from planning to action, helping track progress, measure outcomes, and learn from experience.

Examples

  • Implementation planning
  • Performance metrics
  • Monitoring programs
  • Program evaluation
  • Outcome tracking
  • Adaptive management

Focus Areas

The focus areas below illustrate where we see opportunities to contribute. These are not intended to be rigid programs, but areas where our capabilities may be applied.

Carbon & Energy Systems

Supporting informed decisions related to carbon management, energy systems, and infrastructure development.

Topics include

  • Carbon capture and storage
  • Emissions management
  • Energy infrastructure
  • Infrastructure planning
  • Monitoring
  • Reporting

Environment & Health

Examining the relationships among environmental conditions, natural resources, public health, and community outcomes.

Topics include

  • Environmental quality
  • Natural resources
  • Public health
  • Environmental justice
  • Community well-being

Risk & Resilience

Helping organizations understand risks, evaluate options, and improve long-term resilience.

Topics include

  • Wildfire modeling
  • Hazard assessment
  • Climate adaptation
  • Infrastructure resilience
  • Long-term planning

Communities & Decision-Making

Supporting collaborative processes that help stakeholders understand issues, evaluate options, and take action.

Topics include

  • Stakeholder engagement
  • Public participation
  • Facilitation
  • Surveys
  • Collaborative planning
  • Decision-support processes
